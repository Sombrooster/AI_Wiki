Evolutionary Algorithms

A set of many generated programs are given a set of inputs and the expectation of a certain output. The first “generation” will do poorly initially, but, by random chance, some will outperform their counterparts. The programs who performed best then are used to psuedo-randomly create the next “generation” using their interworking variables as a seed.This cycle is repeated until eventually, a program is created that can perform the task at hand with great accuracy.

Genetic Algorithms

Genetic algorithms are a subsect of evolutionary algorithms. They organize the interworkings into specific “chromosomes” Allows for a more efficient creation process of the next generation by noticing which specific parts work. This difference is also the crux of our next form of machine learning

History

Pre-Computer (13th Century-20th Century_

  • 13th Century: Ramon Lull a Spanish theologian, invented machines for discovering non-mathematical truths through combinatorics.
  • 1642: Pascal created the first mechanical digital calculating machine.
  • 1801: Joseph-Marie Jacquard invented the Jacquard loom, the first programmable machine, instructions for the machine were on punched cards.

Turing Test (1950)

Developed by Alan Turing(1912 - 1954) in 1950, in which a human evaluator would judge the test. Knowing that he is communicating with a human on one side and a machine on the other side. All three parties are separated and limited to text. The evaluator is only supposed to look at how close answers are to an answer that a human would give, the correctness of the answer does not matter.If the evaluator cannot tell the machine from the human, then the machine has passed the test.

To Today (1956 - Today)

  • 1956: John McCarthy cointed term 'Artificial Intelligence' as the topic of the Dartmouth Conference, the first conference devoted to the studies of A.I.
  • 1997: IBM’s Deep Blue System defeated the world champion of chess
  • 1997: NASA’s pathfinder mission made a successful landing and the first autonomous robotics system, Sojourner, was deployed on the surface of Mars.
  • 2011: Natural Language Processing: Siri, the first of its kind was a major step forward in A.I.
  • 2011: IBM's Watson won Jeopardy! game-show.
  • 2014: “Chatbot” tricked judges during the Turing test into thinking it was a real human with skin, blood, organs, and everything.
  • 2018: The first time an A.I.-equipped machine flew into space.

Potential Future of AI

Widespread Adoption

The widespread adoption of Artificial Intelligence is one that we will eventually see in our lifetime, and are already somewhat seeing in today's day and age. One example would be the fact that everyones phone most likely has some sort of helping A.I., and we can expect in the future that It will be more complex based on the expectations of A.I.

Integration

The integration in regards to our technology and A.I., is one that will not be as difficult as most people would think.

Positives

  • Self-driving vehicles that are more safe and aware than humans
  • The integration into geological studies in order to predict natural disasters and more.

Negatives

  • An artificial intelligence that can think and act in superior ways than a human can. (This is probably not in our lifetime, as the process of making a artificial intelligence based solely on the neurological pathways in our brains is very complex.
  • The room for human creativity in the technological might be squandered if the artificial intelligence that’s integrated is designed to think for itself and not for the person using it.
